☁️ Tax Residency Rules

Reference guide for Jacky Mao's 2026 tax residency planning

🇦🇪 UAE — Dubai Tax Residency

The UAE has no personal income tax. Maintaining UAE tax residency means Jacky pays $0 in income tax.

Key Rule

Dubai must be Jacky's primary country of residence. He must spend more days in Dubai than in any other single country during the calendar year (Jan 1 – Dec 31).

What Supports UAE Residency

  • Physical presence — most days spent in Dubai
  • Valid Emirates ID
  • Residence visa
  • UAE bank accounts
  • Property ownership or rental in Dubai
  • Entry/exit stamps and boarding pass records

Note: Dubai doesn't need more than 50% of the year — it just needs to be the #1 country by day count, beating each other country individually.

🇨🇦 Canada — CRA Residency Rules

Canada taxes residents on worldwide income. If deemed a Canadian tax resident, Jacky would owe tax on all global income.

The 183-Day Rule

Spending 183 or more days in Canada in a calendar year creates a strong presumption of tax residency.

Days in CanadaRisk Level

Residential Ties Test

Even under 183 days, the CRA can deem residency based on ties:

  • Primary ties: Home (owned or rented), spouse/partner in Canada, dependents
  • Secondary ties: Bank accounts, driver's licence, provincial health card (OHIP), social memberships, mailing address

Recommendation: Sever residential ties — sell or rent out property, close or minimize Canadian bank accounts, cancel provincial health card, update mailing address to Dubai.

🇺🇸 USA — Substantial Presence Test

The IRS uses a weighted 3-year formula to determine if a foreign national is a US tax resident.

The Formula

(Days in current year) + (⅓ × days in prior year) + (⅙ × days in two years prior) ≥ 183

If the result is 183 or more, and the person was in the US for at least 31 days in the current year, they are treated as a US tax resident.

Safe limit: Keep US days under ~120 per year to leave margin for the weighted calculation.

New York State Residency

NY State has its own separate rule:

  • Spending 183+ days in New York State, AND
  • Maintaining a permanent place of abode (apartment, house)
  • = New York State tax resident

Recommendation: Avoid permanent housing in NY — no long-term lease, no owned property. Use hotels or short-term stays.

✈️ Double-Dip / Flight Days

When traveling between two countries on the same calendar day, that day can count toward both countries.

How It Works

  • Leave Toronto Sep 6, arrive NYC Sep 6 → counts as +1 Toronto AND +1 NYC
  • A single calendar day adds to two country totals

🌏 "Other" Country Days

Days spent in countries other than UAE, Canada, or USA (e.g., Vietnam, South Korea, Japan).

  • Do NOT count toward Canada's 183-day threshold
  • Do NOT count toward the US substantial presence test
  • Do NOT count toward Dubai either
  • Too many "Other" days shrink Dubai's lead

Balance: Vacations are fine, but every day in "Other" is a day not in Dubai — it reduces the buffer.
